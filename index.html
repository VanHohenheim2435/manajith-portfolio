Process-first breakdowns

Gwanlinn, The Forgotten City

Role: Level Designer | Solo Project | Tools: Unreal Engine

View Design Document

A compact Soulslike-inspired level designed around exploratory loops, shortcut discovery, and spatial memory through interconnected city structure.

The Problem

The level needed to evoke FromSoftware-style exploration in a smaller scope while still providing progression friction, orientation landmarks, and meaningful route decisions.

Constraints

  • Intro area required to onboard players before the main city space.
  • Main zone had to reward exploration and intentional backtracking.
  • Target completion around 10-15 minutes, depending on combat implementation.

Process & Design Decisions

  • Studied Bloodborne's Cathedral Ward for hub-linked pathing and loop structure.
  • Used landmark-driven composition to support orientation without heavy UI guidance.
  • Added hidden paths and shortcuts to reward curiosity and reduce backtrack fatigue.
  • Structured tension between safe areas and traversal risk to reinforce pace variety.

Playtesting & Iteration

Feedback rounds prioritized navigation clarity, route confidence, and progression smoothness. Adjustments improved discoverability while preserving mystery and non-linear exploration value.

Siege & Shield

Role: Design Lead | Group Project | Tools: Unreal Engine

View Design Document Watch Gameplay Video

A third-person tower defense design that combines base-building strategy with high-mobility movement inspired by Titanfall-style traversal.

The Problem

Traditional tower defense can become static. The design challenge was to preserve strategic depth while introducing active player movement and combat participation.

Constraints

  • Core loop had to combine tower placement, maze planning, and direct combat support.
  • Economy starts at fixed resources to enforce meaningful early decisions.
  • Enemy set includes melee, ranged, tank, and ogre pressure roles.

Process & Design Decisions

  • Defined project vision and maintained the GDD as design lead.
  • Built tower roles (ballista, anti-ogre pylon, cauldron, life, garrison) around lane control.
  • Integrated wall-running and mobility to keep player agency high between build phases.
  • Balanced progression by aligning economy pressure with enemy wave composition.

Playtesting & Iteration

Team feedback loops focused on tower value clarity, difficulty spikes, and traversal readability. Iterations tightened the relationship between macro strategy and moment-to-moment action.

The Abandoned Brewery

Role: Level Designer | Solo Project | Tools: Far Cry 5 Map Editor

View Design Document Watch Gameplay Video

An outpost-level case study built around player choice, supporting both stealth infiltration and direct assault with no single dominant strategy.

The Problem

Outpost encounters often break when one vantage point solves the full space. The challenge was to preserve planning depth and player agency without trivializing combat through long-range safety.

Constraints

  • 5-10 minute average clear time depending on playstyle and skill.
  • Balanced support for loud and stealth routes.
  • Enemy distribution that discourages single-position dominance.

Process & Design Decisions

  • Referenced Far Cry and Assassin's Creed outpost structures to study approach variety.
  • Built layered entry points and interior/exterior pressure zones.
  • Designed scouting spaces at elevation while preserving local counter-threats.
  • Spread enemy archetypes to force movement and tactical adaptation.

Playtesting & Iteration

Test passes focused on stealth readability, combat escalation pacing, and recovery options after failed infiltration. Encounter placements were refined to keep both approaches consistently viable.

As Above, So Below

Role: Level Designer | Solo Project | Tools: Hammer Editor

View Design Document

A competitive King of the Hill map with layered verticality, designed for frequent player encounters while preserving readable routes and fair objective access.

The Problem

The map needed to avoid two common pitfalls: oversized layouts that reduce engagement and cramped layouts that remove tactical breathing room. The objective zone also had to prevent one team from gaining uncontestable control.

Constraints

  • Two-level layout with interconnected central spaces.
  • Multiple objective routes for both teams to support fairness.
  • Clear sniper and flanker opportunities without dominant sightline abuse.

Process & Design Decisions

  • Researched Overwatch 2's Lijiang Tower to benchmark control-map flow and symmetry.
  • Blockout focused on equal travel times and mirrored access pressure to point.
  • Added class-based vertical entries so mobility heroes gain utility without mandatory picks.
  • Positioned health/ammo pickups to extend engagements and encourage team rotations.

Playtesting & Iteration

Iteration cycles targeted route clarity, capture-point contest frequency, and high-ground value. Adjustments were made to reduce dead zones and improve transition pacing between both levels.
